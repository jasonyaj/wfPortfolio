About Me:

For as long as I can remember, I have always loved to break things down and see how I can rebuild them. At an early age I would break my action figures apart and mismatch them. The teenage me would spend countless all-nighters customizing and building characters in video-games just to see how they would perform in their environment. Eventually, I got into card and board games as it allowed me to scratch my itch further for building. Today, I find myself revisiting coding and expanding my enjoyment for building and debugging.


Today, I find myself dividing my time between the outdoors, spending time in the garage wrenching, and working on personal web apps.

Projects:

IKWIG

IKWIG, is the acronym for "I Know What I Got". A responsive virtual garage application that enables users to record and track a personalized library of cars. Current functions include registration and login with verifications. Users are able to add a photo along with their car entry form. Added cars are also moveable from a current car collection to the sold list.

Rescute

A personal goal of mines was to build an "oldschool" computer game similar to the ones I use to run on Windows 95. Rescute is a topdown maze crawler. The object of the gameplay is to rescue all the bunnies in each stage while navigating through the mage. Game progression include harder mazes and increased rescuees as you progress.
